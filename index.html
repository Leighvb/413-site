The Philosophy

What happened to you matters.
It does not get to define you.

Life leaves wreckages. Trauma. Loss. Burnout. Betrayal. Illness. Caregiving. Failure. Most people are handed a script that says: survive, recover, move on. The 413 Movement rejects that script.

Survival is the baseline. It is not the destination. The destination is sovereignty — the complete, unapologetic reclamation of your identity, your voice, and your future.

This is not about toxic positivity. It is not about finding the silver lining. It is about doing the hard, honest, unfiltered work of rebuilding from the inside out — and refusing to pretend that the wreckage wasn't real.

The unseen finally wants to be seen.
The unheard finally wants to be heard.
And I have a roar ready for just the right moment. — Leigh van Baalen

This is that moment.

The Framework

The Four Pillars

Pillar One

Wreckage

Acknowledging the full reality of the crisis. Not minimising it. Not performing recovery. Looking it directly in the face and naming it for what it is.

Pillar Two

Resilience

The persistence to move through the aftermath. Not bouncing back — there is no back. Moving forward, even when forward is just one breath at a time.

Pillar Three

Sovereignty

Reclaiming complete ownership of your identity and your future. Moving from surviving to creating. From victim to author of your own next chapter.

Pillar Four

Unfiltered Truth

No masks. No performance. No toxic positivity. Only the brutal, honest truth that sets you free to build a life entirely on your own terms.

The Founder

Leigh van Baalen

Author. Speaker. Founder of The 413 Movement.

Leigh van Baalen spent 30 years in corporate South Africa — across telecoms, retail, IT, mining, and ERP transformation — before life handed her something no boardroom had prepared her for.

In 2018, her son Kelvin was involved in a paragliding accident that left him fighting for his life. What followed was 413 days in a burns unit — 413 days of living in hospital corridors, navigating a broken system, watching someone you love rebuild from ash.

From that experience came a memoir, a movement, and 413 unfiltered truths that Leigh spent eight years distilling into Survival to Sovereignty.

Her voice is direct. Her perspective is hard-won. Her refusal to pretend it was anything other than what it was — is exactly what makes her worth listening to.

The Origin

Why 413?

413

It is not simply a number. It never was.

The Days

413 is the exact number of days Leigh's son spent in a burns unit following a paragliding accident that changed everything. Every single one of those days counted.

The Truths

413 is also the number of unfiltered, unspoken truths that emerged from living through those days — truths that could only have been found in that fire.

One number. Two meanings. A movement built from both.
